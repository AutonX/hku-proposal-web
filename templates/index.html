Stock Price Prediction

Using Large Language Models for Analysis of Financial News and Reports

Introduction

Introduction

With the rapid development and increasing complexity of global financial markets, predictive analytics and trading have become more significant in the finance sector. Traditional methods of analyzing financial markets face considerable challenges in managing large volumes of historical and real-time data.

Tradition methods often struggle to effectively process and analyze unstructured data scattered across various financial reports and news articles, which are crucial for understanding market dynamics and predicting stock price trends.This project aims to propose a novel approach to stock price prediction by integrating the strengths of time series forecasting and Large Language Models (LLMs), developing a hybrid stock price prediction model.

In this model, time series analysis is responsible for processing and forecasting stock price trends based on historical trading data, while Large Language Models are utilized to deeply analyze financial news and reports. These text data serve as an additional input, potentially enhancing the overall performance of the forecasting model.

technique

LSTM

LSTM is one of the most classic time series price prediction models. It can learn long-term dependencies and maintain information over time, aiding in tasks like sequence prediction and time-series forecasting, It has a good correlation with stock price data. but the current price is not the only factor affecting future prices, so lstm also has certain limitations in stock price prediction.

LLM

LLM is the hottest AI model nowadays. Designed to understand and generate human-like text based on large amounts of training data. In addition to text generation and situational dialogue, it has also recently performed very well in the field of sentiment analysis. Using LLM to do sentiment analysis on company news is a good choice.

Data engineering

We have selected some of the most representative stocks in the US stock market. Within the same time interval, price and news sentiment are mathematically combined, and the final integrated result is used as the input data source for the final stage of stock price prediction.
